The Problem

Students disengage with passive content. Existing AI tools produce the same flat outputs — summaries, Q&A, flashcards — that students skim and forget.

No tool turns a case study or lecture into something students experience.

The Solution

Paste a case study, lecture notes, or a topic. Playable Lessons uses AI to generate a branching interactive narrative students play — making choices, seeing consequences, reaching different endings based on their decisions. Variables like trust, experience, and risk shift with each choice.

Why Playable Lessons

1

Playable, not passive

The only tool that creates branching interactive fiction from source material. Students don't read — they decide, fail, recover, and remember.

2

Seven outputs, one source

One paste generates a story, flashcards, a quiz, a summary, AI-collaboration tasks, and a case study — plus a recommender that picks the right set.

3

Curtin controls the data

Self-hosted Docker instance. Works offline with Ollama. No vendor lock-in, no data sent to third parties, no per-user licensing. Open source (MIT).

Why It Works (Pedagogically)

Active learning through narrative. Research consistently shows that decision-forcing scenarios — where learners face consequences for choices — produce deeper engagement and better retention than passive reading or recall (Kolb's experiential learning; branched-scripting in medical and military training).

AI-collaboration tasks build critical thinking. Not "use AI to summarise X" — tasks designed so the AI's first answer is wrong unless the student probes, challenges, and refines. Load-bearing specifics force interrogation, not delegation.
